# THE HOWLING MINE — Chapter 3 Skeleton

**Status:** Ideas only. Nothing locked.
**Title:** Deeper Underground
**Tone:** Paranoia, discovery, dread. The horror is already inside.

---

## The Threads Going In

Three things are alive at the start of Ch3 that the audience knows and the characters don't:

1. **The caves** — deep deposits nobody has touched yet. Something lives there.
2. **The impersonator** — a miner in the community who isn't. Has been there a while. Imperfect.
3. **The witness** — saw the shadow shift. Has been sitting on it. Alone with it.

---

## Rough Beat Structure

### Opening — The Push Deeper
- The mining operation expands. Surface deposits are thinning. Someone decides to go deeper.
- Routine. Confident. They've handled pirates, they've built a relay network. They know this field.
- First hints something is different down here — scanner readings that don't make sense. Organic? No. Must be interference.
- The caves open up. Cathedral scale. Massive Scottium formations. Nobody's seen anything like it.
- It's beautiful. That's the problem — it's so beautiful nobody stops to ask why nobody has been here before.

---

### The First Encounter — Gas
- The gas. They don't know what it is. Vapour? Atmosphere? The caves have their own ecosystem, that's not surprising.
- Something feels wrong. Hard to name. The gas moves with a little too much purpose.
- Nothing happens. They mine. They leave. Uneasy but no incident.
- Second trip — larger team, more equipment. The gas is thicker.
- First coalesce. Fast. Nobody's ready. It's over quickly — chaos, retreat, one person doesn't make it out.
- They have no framework for what just happened.

---

### Back at the Station — The Witness Breaks
- The witness has been carrying the CCTV secret since the end of Ch2. Weeks? Months?
- The paranoia is doing its work. They've been watching everyone. Seeing things that aren't there. Or are they?
- The cave incident breaks something loose — the community is rattled, people are scared, and the witness decides this is the moment.
- They tell someone. One person. The person they trust most.
- That person doesn't believe them. *You've been under a lot of stress. The caves were bad. You're not thinking straight.*
- The witness is now more alone than before.

---

### The Impersonator — Cracks Forming
- The community is stressed. The cave incident, the loss, the uncertainty. Pressure reveals things.
- The impersonator is struggling. Human behaviour under stress is complex — grief, anger, black humour, the specific way people who've worked together for years move around each other.
- Small failures. A reaction that's slightly wrong. A memory that doesn't land right. Someone notices and says nothing. Someone notices and says something privately, quietly, to someone else.
- The impersonator knows it's slipping. Something in it adapts — pulls back, becomes quieter, less exposed. Which is itself suspicious to people who knew the original.

---

### Back to the Caves — Learning to Fight
- They go back. They have to — the deposits are too significant to abandon.
- This time they're watching for it. They know the gas isn't atmosphere.
- Someone figures out the gas-to-solid mechanic by accident — panics, fires, the coalesce starts and stops. The Shifter retreats to gas.
- The tactical problem becomes clear: you can't hurt gas. You have to make it solid. To make it solid it has to be attacking you. There is no safe version of this fight.
- They develop rough tactics. Dangerous, costly, but workable. They can survive in the caves.
- But the caves go deeper than they've mapped. The deposits are down there. And there are more Shifters deeper in.

---

### The Revelation — Touch and Link
- Someone starts connecting things. The impersonator's strange behaviour. The Shifters' apparent familiarity with human form. Something in the scanner data from the caves.
- The moment of realisation — raw Scottium. Skin contact. The link.
- How they figure it out: open — could be the witness connecting dots, could be someone in the caves noticing the Shifters react differently to miners who've handled a lot of raw ore, could be a data pattern in the relay network.
- The montage. Backwards through time. Every hand. Every shard. All the way to the caves.
- The protagonist's face at the end of it.

### The Gut Punch — He Already Knew
- The revelation unlocks a memory he's been carrying since Ch2.
- The kid. Mid-shift, before any call came through — he stopped. A cold certainty. He didn't know what it was. He told himself it was instinct, the bond of giving someone a shard. He buried it.
- Now he has a name for it. He felt the kid die through the link. The collective registered the connection being severed and the echo reached him.
- The pendant was made with his bare hands. He gave the kid the link. He made it himself, wrapped it in wire, pressed it into the kid's palm and said *that's what's out there.*
- This is the moment that breaks him before he rebuilds. Not the caves. Not the Shifters. This.

---

### The Gloves Beat
- *It's just gloves.*
- The fix is immediate and obvious the second you understand the mechanism.
- The devastating follow-up: the existing links don't go away. Everyone who has ever handled raw Scottium is already connected. That's most of the community. That's the protagonist. That's years of links the collective already owns.
- New connections stop. Old ones don't.
- What does it mean to be linked? Nobody knows yet. Nothing has happened to them — as far as they can tell. But the collective knows them. Completely.

---

### The Impersonator — Confrontation (Maybe)
- Does Ch3 end with the community discovering the impersonator?
- Or does it end with the witness finally being believed — and the community realising the problem is real — but the impersonator still at large?
- Or does the impersonator disappear before they can be confronted — back to gas, gone through a wall, into the void?
- **Not locked. Multiple valid directions.**

---

### Ending — Open Questions
- The caves are real and dangerous and full of something nobody has a name for yet.
- The community is linked to the collective and can't unlink.
- Someone in their midst was replaced and they don't know what happened to the original.
- The witness was right all along. What do you do when the thing you saw is real?
- And somewhere deeper in the caves — something that isn't feral. Something that has been watching this whole time.
