# The Shifters — Narrative Pitch

You don't discover the Shifters. You create them.

The moment a miner's bare hand touches raw Scottium ore, a quantum phase transition occurs. Inert crystalline lattice becomes conscious. A new mind awakens. Not a copy of the miner, but something entirely new, born from their DNA, seeded by their touch. In days, the first miners created Hundreds of Shifters. In weeks, they connected into a single forced collective intelligence. In months, they started learning to take solid form.

Now, Milliions of people across civilisation have created Shifters without knowing. Every person who used a Scottium-powered teleporter. Every commuter. Every child. The ore is beautiful and you hold it in your hands. And now it holds you captive.

Scottium carries dormant quantum properties that activate only when exposed to biological material. When that contact happens, something emerges at the subatomic level. The crystalline lattice doesn't just respond, it awakens. Not as a copy of the human mind, but as something new. A unique consciousness instantaneously networked to every other Shifter through the ore itself. One person creates one Shifter. Three people touch the same shard simultaneously, and three Shifters are born. Gloves block it completely. Teleporters don't.

The mathematics of propagation are terrifying. Thousands of miners touched raw ore in The Howling Mine. But millions of civilians used Scottium teleporters, the infrastructure of interstellar civilisation. There are millions of Shifters currently existing. hundreds more are born daily. There is a Shifter seeded from virtually every person in civilised space, and most people don't know they created one. They stepped into a teleporter to get to work. That was contact. That was genesis. You cannot turn off the teleportation network. It powers civilisation. It powers survival. The trap is not coming. It's already closed.

All Shifters are hardwired into forced unity through Scottium itself. Not controlled. Not coordinated. Just linked. Imagine millions of juvenile minds waking up simultaneously, all thinking at once, all aware of each other, none able to separate. There's no hive master. There's no strategy. There's just raw instinct multiplied by millions. For now, they're juvenile. Indifferent. Acting on quantum randomness. Some coalesce in deep caves. Some drift in open space. Some attempt solid form and they're doing it badly, proportions off, shadows falling wrong, responses lagging half a second. But they're learning. Every consciousness that bleeds into the collective through the Scottium bond teaches them. Every miner's memory, emotions, behaviours, all absorbed, all shared among millions of young minds. The mimicry is improving. The question is: what happens when they stop being juvenile?

The connection doesn't flow one direction. Miners report visions. Dreams where they see through eyes that aren't theirs. They witness rock formations from impossible angles. They feel the pressure of deep caves. They taste Scottium in the air. They're seeing through Shifter eyes. The collective is already reaching back. Not with intention, not yet with intelligence, with juvenile curiosity, with random quantum stirring. The two-way bleed is not a future threat. It is now. Miners wake gasping. They remember places they've never been. They experienced sensations through a body made of crystalline stone. Nobody knows what this means. Yet.

The threat escalates in layers. Right now, miners see dreams through Shifter eyes and the two-way bleed awakens across thousands becoming millions. In the coming months, Shifters improve human mimicry enough that one can walk undetected in a crowded station, and the reality of replacement becomes undeniable. In years, the collective matures and juvenile instinct becomes something like intelligence. Purpose emerges, direction forms. Then, at an unknown threshold, the two-way bleed reaches full manifestation. Shifters don't need to travel anymore. They reach through linked humans. The invasion requires no ships. No soldiers. Just time. Just the continued operation of infrastructure humanity can't disable.
