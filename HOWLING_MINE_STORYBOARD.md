## SCENE 1: THE YEARNING, "Some of us yearn for something more"

**Setting:** Setesh Moon, cyberpunk tutorial area. Neon signage, industrial structures, gritty and soulless. A place people pass through, not stay.

**Narrative Context:** Our narrator is looking back. He's already made it. But he's telling us about the beginning, the version of him on screen doesn't know what's coming yet.

**Film Theory:** Campbell's "Ordinary World." The mundane before the Call to Adventure. The camera stays low and trapped, visually earthbound. Tarkovsky's slow burn, patience here earns the payoff later. Kuleshov Effect, the VO tells us one thing (longing) while visuals show another (routine), and the gap is the emotional engine.

**Film Theory:** Campbell's threshold crossing. The hero commits to the unknown. The framing shifts from low and trapped to centred and purposeful. The character has agency now.

**Tonal Shift:** Pacing quickens slightly. Music gains a pulse. VO shifts from wistful reflection to quiet determination.
