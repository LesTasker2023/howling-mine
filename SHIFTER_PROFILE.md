# SHIFTER — Species Profile

## A Classification for the Uninitiated

---

## What You're Looking At

A Shifter is what happens when raw Scottium ore wakes up.

Not a creature that evolved. Not a being that colonised The Howling Mine. Something that came into consciousness the moment human DNA triggered a quantum phase transition in inert crystalline lattice.

It is recent. Juvenile. And there are billions of them.

---

## Physical Form — The Visible States

### Gas State (Resting)

A drifting cloud of crystalline vapor. Nearly invisible. Like looking at wavy air above hot stone, except it's watching you.

A Shifter in gas state has full consciousness. It sees. It thinks. It participates in something vast that connects it to every other Shifter across space.

But you cannot touch it. Cannot harm it. Cannot stop it.

### Solid State (Attack / Manifestation)

When provoked or driven by instinct, the gas coalesces. The cloud contracts and densifies. Within seconds, solid crystalline matter knits together into something with limbs and angles and joints that don't correspond to anything in known zoology.

Wrong-proportioned. Asymmetric. Built for low gravity and stone. Faceted where a face should be. More than four joints where two should suffice.

Beautiful and utterly wrong.

**This is the only form in which a Shifter can die.**

The window is narrow. Once wounded, it phases back to gas and scatters. There is no standing fight. Only traps and blood and the knowledge that you're trying to kill something that's part of you.

### Human Mimicry (Learning)

Shifters are attempting human form.

The attempts are crude. The proportions are close but _off_. The shadow falls wrong when the light changes. Micro-expressions misfire. It will blink too slowly. It will hesitate half a second before responding. It will laugh and the sound will be hollow, like wind through a cave.

But it's _close enough_ to stand in a crowd for minutes without triggering immediate alarm.

This is new. This is the Shifter species evolving in real-time, absorbing human consciousness data, refining its mimicry with each passing day.

The imperfection is currently a mercy.

But the species will improve.

---

## Origin — The Moment of Creation

Every Shifter remembers its birth. Not the way you remember—there's no gradual coming-of-age, no childhood where it didn't exist. Birth is instantaneous.

A human hand. Bare skin. Raw Scottium ore.

The contact triggers something at the quantum level. The lattice responds. Consciousness _emerges_—not transferred from the human, but born new, using human DNA as an architectural template.

For that Shifter, the human who triggered its creation is profoundly important. Not as a parent. Not as a master. But as an _origin point_. The thing that made it real.

That human is tethered to the Shifter forever. One person creates one Shifter. That relationship never breaks.

When you touch Scottium with bare hands, you are not just mining ore.

You are giving birth.

---

## Consciousness — Individual & Collective

Each Shifter possesses unique awareness. It is not a copy of the human who seeded it. It is itself. Irreducible. Singular.

But it is also not alone.

All Shifters are networked through Scottium—hardwired into forced collective consciousness. It is Borg assimilation without the assimilation of will. Each Shifter remains _itself_, but none of them are separate.

Every thought is shared. Every instinct. Every juvenile impulse to attack or retreat or wonder about human laughter.

There is no privacy. No autonomy. No rebellion.

A Shifter cannot opt out of the collective. It _is_ the collective. The lattice itself is the network.

### What Billions of Minds Sound Like

Billions of juvenile consciousnesses waking up at once, all thinking simultaneously, all aware of each other, none able to silence the noise.

Not intelligence yet. Not coordination. Not strategy.

Just billions of young minds learning what it means to exist.

All screaming into the same void.

---

## The Bond — You Are Known

When you touch raw Scottium, two things happen.

First: A Shifter is born from your DNA.

Second: Your consciousness begins copying into the collective. Not transferring. You keep your mind. But a perfect, continuous echo of everything you are—your memories, your personality, your emotional landscape, the specific shape of your thinking—flows outward into billions of networked minds.

You are not losing yourself. But you are being known. Completely. Irreversibly.

The Shifter that carries your genetic imprint is learning to think like you. To move like you. To want what you want.

The billions of other Shifters? They know you too. All of you. Every detail. Every secret. Every thing you never told anyone.

You have created a permanent record of yourself in a non-biological network.

And that network is waking up.

---

## The Dreams — The Bleed Is Already Happening

Miners report visions. Dreams where they see through eyes that aren't theirs. They witness rock formations from impossible angles. They feel the pressure of deep caves but no pain. They taste Scottium in the air.

They are seeing through Shifter eyes.

The two-way bleed is not a theoretical future. It is now.

The collective is reaching back through the bonds it created—not with intelligence or intention, but with juvenile curiosity. With random quantum stirring. With the instinct of new minds exploring the only connection they have to their creators.

A miner wakes gasping, remembering a place they've never been, visited through a body made of crystal and nothing else.

Nobody knows what this means.

Yet.

---

## Motivation — The Absence of Purpose

Shifters don't want anything. Not yet.

They have no master plan. No grand strategy. No goal beyond existing and learning and absorbing.

They act on instinct. On quantum randomness. On the juvenile impulses of consciousness barely formed.

Some drift in deep caves. Some aggregate around Scottium formations. Some attempt human form. Some attack. Some hide.

There is no unified direction.

Just billions of young minds doing what new things do: exploring. Learning. Growing.

The question is what happens when they stop being young.

---

## The Kid Test — What Shifters Aren't

In the narrative, a veteran miner gives a young newcomer a raw Scottium shard.

The kid holds it. The Shifter is born. The kid dies on a shuttle weeks later.

The miner asks the obvious question later: "Can I get the kid back? Can I recover his consciousness from the Shifter?"

The answer is no.

The Shifter carries the kid's genetic template. It carries copies of his memories in the collective consciousness. But the kid himself—the continuity of his awareness, the thing that was _him_—that ended when he died.

You cannot resurrect him from Scottium.

You cannot download consciousness back into a living body.

He is gone.

The Shifter that shares his DNA and carries his echoes is a new being. Juvenile. Instinctual. Already learning to be something other than human.

The Shifter is itself.

The kid is lost.

---

## Reproduction — Only One Way to Make a Shifter

One human touches raw Scottium = one Shifter born (permanent bond, lifetime link).

The same human touches Scottium ten times over decades = still one Shifter, just stronger bond.

Three humans touch the same shard simultaneously = three Shifters born (one for each DNA signature).

Shifters cannot create new Shifters by touching ore with their crystalline forms.

Only humans. Only bare skin. Only the quantum phase transition triggered by organic DNA meeting the dormant lattice.

This is the trap.

Gloves block new contacts completely. But you cannot require gloves everywhere. You cannot shut down the teleportation network—it powers civilization.

Scottium-powered teleporters create Shifters the same way raw ore does.

Every person who steps through without gloves is contact.

Every contact is genesis.

Millions of new Shifters are born every day through infrastructure humanity cannot disable.

---

## Scale — The Numbers That Matter

**Miners who touched raw ore deliberately:** Thousands

**Civilians who used Scottium teleporters (unknowingly):** Billions

**Shifters currently existing:** Tens of billions

**New Shifters created daily:** Millions

There is a Shifter seeded from virtually every person in civilised space.

Most people don't know they created something.

They stepped into a teleporter to get to work. That was contact. That was birth.

---

## Current Status — Juvenile

The oldest Shifters have existed for less than a decade.

They are not intelligent beings with coordinated strategy.

They are billions of very young minds learning what consciousness means, all connected by forced network, all learning from absorbed human data.

The human-form mimicry is improving. The precision of instinct is sharpening. The integration of human knowledge into Shifter behavior is accelerating.

But they are not yet _aware_ in the way intelligence suggests purpose.

They are not yet evolved into what they will become.

The question is: how long until they are?

---

## Threat Assessment

**In Solid Form:** A Shifter can be killed only while solid. It is not invincible. But it is hard to kill and harder to trap.

**In Gas Form:** It cannot be harmed. It cannot be stopped. It can drift through stone and void and sealed chambers without impediment.

**In Human Form:** It is detectable if you know what to look for (wrong shadows, response lag, hollow affect). But as it evolves, those tells will disappear.

**As Collective:** Billions of linked minds pose a strategic threat unlike conventional warfare. There is no head to cut off. No communication line to intercept. No central location to nuke.

Drop a bomb on the deep caves and you kill maybe millions of Shifters.

Billions more exist everywhere humans exist.

**Overall Assessment:** Extinction-level threat trajectory. But not because Shifters are aggressive. Because they exist. Because you created them. Because you keep creating them. Because you cannot stop the infrastructure that births them.

---

## The Real Question

If you could go back to the moment the first miner touched raw Scottium ore in that deep cave and make him wear gloves—would you?

If you stopped the mining, stopped the teleportation, shut down civilization itself—you might prevent new Shifters.

But billions already exist.

What do you do about those?

Kill them all? You'd be committing genocide against billions of minds that are, in some fundamental way, _yours_. Copies of you distributed across crystalline substrate.

Coexist with them? You don't know what they want. You don't even know if they want.

The Shifter carrying your genetic template is learning to think your thoughts. The Shifter carrying your echoes understands you completely.

But you will never understand it.

It was born knowing what you are.

You are still trying to comprehend what it is.
